PKR Bot - CLI Command Reference

Overview

PKR Bot recognizes text-based commands in Microsoft Teams when you mention @PKR. You can add flags (title, time, etc.) for advanced usage.

Example:

@PKR begin title "My Round Title" time 60

Make sure Teams recognizes your mention (the bot name should appear in purple or show the PKR icon).

Commands

CommandPurpose
begin Start a new planning poker round. Accepts flags for customizing the session (see below).
history Search and display past rounds. Accepts flags like limit, startDate, endDate, legacy, etc.
config Shows an interactive configuration card to set your default scale, timer, anonymity, etc.
help Shows a help card with quick usage tips.

Flags for begin

FlagDescriptionExample
title Sets a custom round title (e.g., a story name or ticket reference). @PKR begin title "User Login Story"
time Sets the timer (seconds) before the bot closes the round. Default=30s. @PKR begin time 60
participants or players Forces participant count if Teams membership is not accurate or partial. @PKR begin participants 5

Flags for history

FlagDescriptionExample
limit Number of past rounds to show. Default=10. @PKR history limit 20
startDate / endDate Restrict results to a date range, formatted YYYY-MM-DD. @PKR history startDate 2025-06-01 endDate 2025-06-30
limitToThisChat Restrict results to the current conversation or channel only. @PKR history limitToThisChat true
legacy Includes older v1 data from the pkr-legacy table in the search. @PKR history legacy
searchTitle Fuzzy search by round title substring. @PKR history searchTitle "Backend"

Combining Flags

You can combine multiple flags in one command:

@PKR begin title "XYZ Feature" time 45 participants 6

The bot will parse all flags in order. Similarly, for history:

@PKR history startDate 2025-01-01 endDate 2025-12-31 limit 25

Tips & Troubleshooting

  • Always mention @PKR so Teams routes your text to the bot.
  • Use quotes around multi-word titles, e.g. title "Bug Fix - Payment Flow".
  • If you see “Unrecognized command,” check your syntax or mention @PKR help.
