How to Run a Round of Planning Poker with PKRbot

This page provides step-by-step instructions on how to use PKRbot for your Agile or Scrum estimation rounds in Microsoft Teams.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Invite PKRbot to Your Team Chat

Make sure PKRbot is already installed in your Microsoft Teams environment. If it’s not, please follow the Installation instructions. Once installed, invite PKRbot to the specific channel or group chat where you and your team discuss tasks or user stories.

Step 2: Use the “Begin” Command

In the chat where PKRbot is present, type @PKR begin. This will bring up a card allowing you to configure a new planning poker round. You can specify a custom title, round timer, or estimation scale.

Step 3: Fill in Round Details

PKRbot supports multiple estimation scales (like points, fibonacci, and t-shirt) plus optional confidence scoring. You can also choose how long the estimation round should stay open. Once satisfied, click Start Round.

Step 4: Submit Your Estimates

Every participant in the chat should see a submission card with an input for their estimate (plus an optional confidence input if you enabled it). Each user fills in their estimate before time runs out.

Step 5: Review the Results

When time is up or everyone has submitted, PKRbot replaces the submission card with a results card. This card shows the mean and standard deviation of all numeric estimates. If confidence was enabled, you’ll also see the average confidence and its stddev.

Step 6: Re-Estimate or Comment to Jira

If you linked your Jira ticket in the config, you can click “Comment results to Jira” right from the results card to post an update back to your Jira issue. Or click “Re-estimate Ticket” to quickly start another round if you need to refine your estimates further.

That’s it! You’ve successfully run a planning poker round with PKRbot. For more commands, type @PKR help or check the Usage Docs.
